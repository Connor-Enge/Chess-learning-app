A track-based curriculum for a self-directed adult learner who already knows how the pieces move and basic rules. Goal: take Connor from rules-only to a confident club-level strategist.

## Tracks (the seven)

The app organizes lessons into seven tracks. Each has its own filtered view in the top nav. **Master Games** is also a top-level section, but it's a viewer for annotated classics rather than a lesson track per se.

| Track | What it teaches | # lessons (initial target) |
|---|---|---|
| **Calculation** | The thinking process: evaluation, candidate moves, CCT, blunder check, time management, visualization, forcing-line technique, the quiet-move check | 8 |
| **Openings** | Meta-skills: principles, repertoire choice, study method, traps, transitions to middlegame | 5 |
| **Tactics** | Pattern recognition. Deep per-tactic lessons (5+ embedded puzzles each) plus advanced motifs | 9 deep + 6 advanced = 15 |
| **Positional Play** | Quiet, judgment-based concepts: pawn structure weaknesses, outposts, good/bad bishops, prophylaxis, weak squares, color complexes, maneuvering, restriction, the initiative | 18â€“23 |
| **Exchanges & Piece Values** | "What to trade for what." Piece values, B vs N, the bishop pair, exchange sacrifices, queen sacrifices, the trade checklist | 12 |
| **Endgame** | Conceptual lessons: K+P opposition, rule of square, Lucena, Philidor, rook activity, opposite bishops, basic mates | 7 |
| **Master Games** *(viewer, not a track)* | 25+ annotated classics: Opera Game, Immortal, Evergreen, Capablanca, Karpov, Fischer, Kasparov, Carlsen | 27 games shipped |

**Initial library target: 75+ lessons** plus the master-games collection.

## Suggested learning sequence (6-week plan)

6. **Week 6**: Positional Play track (the rest) + Master Games (one game / day, predict moves, compare)
